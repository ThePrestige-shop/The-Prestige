Here's how to join the fight

Change Your Perception

Vultures are not evil or associated with evil spirits. Vultures play a useful role of removing carcasses and disease they contain from spreading.

Confront Perpetrators

Confront and report any perpetrator stealing vulture eggs or hunting adults. We are all stakeholders to ensure our vultures are safe. Don't stay quiet. Harassing perpetrator can achieve the goal of saving vultures.

Support Vulture Research

A great way to contribute is to support local and international bodies that work to conserve vultures. Funds you give help in ecological research and awareness to help spread positive things about vultures, changing attitudes and percetions.

JOIN INTERNATIONAL VULTURE AWARENESS DAY (IVAD) 2025 CELEBRATION

Vultures have their special day, too. The International Vulture Awareness Day is celebrated every year on the first Saturday in September. For 2025, the day falls on September 6, 2025. As part of activities to celebrate the day in Ghana, the Rufford Foundation is supporting an awareness campaign for small and large scale stakeholders. Which means YOU! Come join us to celebrate and share the importance of vultures with all.

To learn more, visit the website link or WhatsApp us on how you can join and contribute.
