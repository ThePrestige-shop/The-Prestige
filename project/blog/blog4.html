Hooded vulture project in the Cape Coast metropolis

Community outreach as some sites of importance to the the critically endangered hooded vulture in the Cape Coast metropolis, December 16, 2023

In a dynamic collaboration with Radio Central, representatives Joseph Afrifa, Joseph Kobina Daniels, and Samuel Tamekloe embarked on an enlightening talk show to unravel the mysteries surrounding Ghana's vulture population. This initiative, made possible through the Rufford grant we secured, aimed to amplify community awareness and dispel prevalent misconceptions about these majestic birds. Our team, led by Project Lead Joseph Afrifa, engaged with the articulate talk show host Yoel at Ghana Broadcasting Corporation in the Central Region. Together, we delved into the crucial role vultures play in the local ecosystem, emphasizing their significance in maintaining ecological balance. By shedding light on their positive contributions, we sought to challenge misconceptions that often cast vultures in a negative light. The talk show served as a powerful platform for community outreach, fostering a deeper understanding of the importance of vultures in Ghana. Through compelling dialogue and shared insights, we aimed to inspire a collective commitment to vulture conservation, ensuring a harmonious coexistence between these magnificent birds and the communities they inhabit. This venture exemplifies our dedication to environmental education and underscores the impact that collaborative initiatives can have on fostering positive change.
